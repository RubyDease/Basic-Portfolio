About Me

This is my story… Borne and raised in Canada, I’ve spent my time in high school deciding what am I going to do for a carrier. I’ve started very early with my adult life after I’ve graduated for high school, like I didn’t have time time to loose. I went through motherhood, workforce and continuing my education at night for many years. Funny to say I’ve started at the time a computer science degree, remember coding Dbase with a commodore 18K – but since my brain was too young at the time to understand coding, I jumped into other type of sciences “chemistry”- specialized in Oil/petroleum.

I guess my comfort zone is “change”- there is always someone out there telling me “are you crazy!” ...when I comment about my next step – what I’m going to do. Between working and studding, taking care of a kid/house and others at the same time, I finally made it and graduate.

I made a carrier as a chemist for about 10 years in different industries until I decided that I needed to try something else. I always feel that I need something new to learn. My next endeavor, project management- I was told once again “are you crazy …why going back to school you already have a job!!!”.... It got worse…I went for a certificate while I was refining my skills, getting more experience in the field and pursued my PMP certification.

I’ve being working in that field for the last 12 years in different industries including optical components, entertainment /digital media and technology. Today, I guess my next endeavor before my golden age is web development, I’m sure this new skill will get me to where I wanted to do in life in combination with my other skills. And guess what ...the “are you crazy” came out again…. It doesn’t matter if I leave in Montreal, California, North Carolina all the same. I figured is not the people, its me …. I’m really a crazy person.

What I’ve learned from the efforts that I’ve made thought out this year is that by making the effort and getting out of my comfort zone all the jobs that I got through out my carrier were because of what I’ve learned and not because I knew someone in the industry /company. That’s what crazy people do...
